## 3.3 Scope and Safety
Objective:
- Materially shrink default dump and bundle payloads by replacing literal serialization of cold archive bodies with smart history-tier routing.
- Preserve broad repo and history awareness through recent full bodies, checkpoint full bodies, metadata-only cold history, and exact-file re-include paths.
- Make `dump`, `bundle`, and `prune` read one shared history policy so profile behavior, pressure reporting, and future retention work do not drift.

In scope:
- ADD a shared history policy manifest for `dump`, `bundle`, and `prune`.
- UPDATE `ops/bin/dump` so default scope dumps classify history into exact tiers such as:
  - live heads / active packet surfaces: full body
  - recent archive window: full body
  - checkpoint archive window: full body
  - cold archive history: metadata-only entry rather than literal file body
- KEEP metadata-only cold history useful. Each cold entry must still expose enough identity for reasoning and later exact inclusion, for example path, surface class, packet or trace identity when available, and approximate size.
- UPDATE `ops/lib/scripts/bundle.sh` and `ops/lib/manifests/BUNDLE.md` so bundle profiles route against the same history tiers instead of inheriting raw traversal alone.
- UPDATE profile defaults so:
  - analyst gets broad but shallow history awareness
  - architect gets active routing context plus recent relevant history
  - audit stays narrow and proof-first
  - no profile loses exact-file re-include ability for a needed cold leaf
- UPDATE `ops/bin/prune` and `ops/lib/manifests/PRUNE.md` so prune reports whole-repo pressure and dump-visible pressure using the same shared history tiers.
- KEEP automatic prune action bounded. This packet may reduce dump-visible disposable artifacts and may report cold canonical pressure, but it must not auto-delete canonical archive history under an unreviewed blanket rule.
- UPDATE docs/specs/manual and smoke coverage so the history-tier model is explicit and tested.
- UPDATE `storage/dp/active/allowlist.txt` only for tracked files touched by this packet.

Out of scope:
- Changes to analyst, architect, contractor, or audit stance text.
- Canonical archive deletion policy beyond explicit reportable classification and operator or later-packet discretion.
- Git history rewrite.
- New external services or background daemons.
- Renaming ingest or handoff surfaces.

Safety and invariants:
- Default dump must remain truthful about omitted cold bodies; metadata-only substitution must be explicit, never silent.
- Exact-file include must remain available for cold history that becomes relevant.
- Audit proof surfaces must not be degraded by history-tier compaction.
- Shared history policy must be one source of truth, not separate ad hoc logic in dump, bundle, and prune.
- The result must be materially smaller in default dump and bundle payloads; if the reduction is not obvious in before/after measurements, the packet is incomplete.
