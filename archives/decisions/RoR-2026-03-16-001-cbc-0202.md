## CbC Preflight Questions

**Q1: Is structural prevention viable instead of detection?**
Partially. The repo cannot structurally prevent filing-doctrine drift or closeout breakage by construction, so `tools/verify.sh` remains necessary. The preventable part was broad certify replay selection. That is now structural: `ops/lib/manifests/VERIFY.md` defines lane ownership, reason class, registry row, and decision leaf so certify does not guess.

**Q2: Does the enforcement complexity exceed 100 lines without justification?**
No. The added complexity replaces hardcoded lane branching in `tools/verify.sh` plus blunt verify rewriting in `ops/bin/certify` with one policy surface and deterministic selection logic. The payoff is concrete: fewer redundant replay lanes, less certify wall time, and clearer receipt truth.

**Q3: Was structural prevention identified as viable but deferred?**
Yes. A detached new hierarchy for lane importance was rejected. Existing registries (`binaries`, `lint`, `test`) now carry `Infra Importance`, and verify consumes that canon metadata directly.

**Q4: Complexity score (1–5) and rationale:**
Score: 3
Keep. `tools/verify.sh` remains the runtime orchestrator, but certify replay is narrowed to `closeout-critical` lanes plus `packet-local` lanes whose path ownership matches the active packet. This removes disproportional replay without removing the repo-wide full-verify path.

**Q5: Does a SoP note record any deferred structural prevention decision?**
Yes. DP-OPS-0202 closeout records that full-repo verify remains standalone and that future lane additions must be justified through the shared verify policy plus registries instead of ad hoc certify growth.

## Decision

Keep `tools/verify.sh` and `ops/bin/certify`, but make lane selection policy-driven. Certify replay must use `ops/lib/manifests/VERIFY.md`, registry-backed `Infra Importance`, and a certify-generated changed-path file. Full repo verify remains available as a standalone operator-facing action and must not be silently embedded back into every certify run.

## Consequence

Any future certify lane retained or added must carry an owner, reason class, registry path, infra-importance lookup, and decision leaf reference in `ops/lib/manifests/VERIFY.md`. Re-expanding certify replay without updating that policy is a contract violation, not a convenience tweak.
