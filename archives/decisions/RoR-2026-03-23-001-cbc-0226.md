## Context

Addendum A to DP-OPS-0226 uncovered a second closeout-chain drift after the
freshness-proof and certify-replay repairs landed:

- `ops/bin/certify` still treated a real `OPEN-*.txt` artifact as the spine-grade
  trace anchor for `STELA_TRACE_ID`.
- `ops/lib/scripts/bundle.sh` had diverged and was emitting bundle-local
  pseudo-OPEN metadata with a synthesized trace id instead of consuming a real
  current OPEN artifact.
- In collaborator and bundle-first flows this made closeout success depend on
  ambient state such as a stale prior OPEN artifact or an inherited env trace,
  which is not a truthful or structural anchor path.

The addendum needed to preserve the Auditor -> Audit rename mission on the same
branch while repairing the transport and certify chain now, not as a later
cleanup packet.

## CbC Preflight Questions

**Q1: Is structural prevention viable instead of detection?**
Yes. The clean prevention is to keep OPEN as the sole real trace anchor and make
bundle consume or refresh a real current OPEN artifact rather than synthesize
parallel transport-only trace metadata.

**Q2: Does the enforcement complexity exceed 100 lines without justification?**
No. The repair is moderate and justified because it removes a split-brain
contract across `open`, `bundle`, `certify`, smoke tests, and operator docs.

**Q3: Was structural prevention identified as viable but deferred?**
No. Deferring would leave collaborator and rerun flows exposed to opportunistic
success from stale OPEN state, which is exactly the class of drift the addendum
was authorized to eliminate.

**Q4: Complexity score (1–5) and rationale:**
Score: 3. The repair touches multiple linked surfaces, but the governing rule is
simple: one real anchor, one current branch/head match, no pseudo-anchor path.

**Q5: Does a SoP note record any deferred structural prevention decision?**
No deferral is recorded. The follow-through is included in this addendum closeout
because the transport-chain repair is part of making DP-OPS-0226 certifiable.

## Decision

Keep `ops/bin/open` as the sole spine-grade trace-anchor source. Make
`ops/lib/scripts/bundle.sh` consume a current real OPEN artifact for the active
branch/head, refreshing one through `ops/bin/open` when absent or stale. Do not
permit bundle to invent standalone pseudo-OPEN trace ids. Update smoke coverage,
verification routing, and specs in lockstep so the repaired chain is enforced
structurally.

## Consequence

Collaborator and bundle-first flows no longer rely on stale ambient OPEN state
or synthesized bundle-local trace metadata. The addendum can close truthfully on
the same branch, and future receipt/audit cycles do not have to rediscover this
trace-anchor split.
